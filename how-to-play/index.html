How to Play

A kanji quiz Japanese kids actually use — now free for anyone learning Japanese

This is a quiz I built for my own kids to learn kanji. It's free, runs in your browser, and there's no login. The whole app is in Japanese with furigana, so it works best once you can read kana — think of it as light immersion.

👤 Best for: learners who can already read hiragana (roughly JLPT N5–N4). It covers the kanji Japanese children learn in grades 1–3.

How to play

  1. Pick a quiz and choose a grade (1–3) and how many questions.
  2. Read the short sentence. One kanji is missing.
  3. Tap the kanji that fits the meaning of the sentence.
  4. You get instant feedback, plus the meaning of every choice and the kanji's readings.

What makes it different

Most apps drill kanji in isolation. Here you learn them in context, by meaning — including the same-reading kanji that confuse everyone:

  • 海 / 回 / 貝 — all read "kai", but very different.
  • 会う / 合う — both "au", but used differently.

Keep going

  • 📖 Collect kanji in a picture book as you get them right.
  • 🏅 Earn up to 100 medals and build a daily streak.
  • 🔁 A review mode brings back the ones you missed.
  • Clear grades 1–2 to unlock grade 3.
